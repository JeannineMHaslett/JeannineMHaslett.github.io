Take a ride on the Rail Runner

100 Rail Runner Dr.
Belen, NM 87002
Website: Railrunner Train Fares

Built in 2006, the Rail Runner commuter train originally went from Belen to Bernalillo, just north of Albuquerque, with stops in Los Lunas, Isleta and Albuquerque. Later, it was extended all the way to Santa Fe. It soon became popular with college students and those who work in Albuquerque. Schools use it for field trips, and families use it for trips to the Albuquerque Bioparks, Old Town and museums, as the train station is within walking distance.

Train fares depend on distance traveled, as there are several stops between Belen and Santa Fe. Single passes and monthly passes are available. See the website to purchase tickets and for further details.


Through the Flower Art Gallery

107 Becker Avenue
Belen, NM 87002
505-864-4080
Website: Through the Flower Art Space

Did you know that Judy Chicago moved to Belen a few years ago? She opened up a gallery just west of the Harvey House Museum in the historical district between First and Second Streets. If you are planning to visit the gallery, you will need to book a viewing in advance. Due to COVID restrictions, only groups of 5 or less are allowed in for 30 minute time slots. Call or go online to book an appointment.

Through The Flower art Gallery focuses on art specifically pertaining to women.


Harvey House Museum

104 N First Street
Belen, NM 87002
505-861-0581
Website: Harvey House Museum
Special Events--Facebook

The Belen Harvey House operated from 1910 to 1939, and briefly reopened in the 1940's during World War 2 to serve troop trains.In the 1950's it became a dormitory for railroad employees. That continued through the 1970's. These days it is a museum that hosts all kinds of events from quilting shows to the Christmas Bugg lights display (named after Norman Bugg who donated most of the displays after his enormous display became too much for him and his wife to handle in their golden years). Admission is free, but donations are accepted and welcome.Don't forget to sign the guest book! For more historical information, see

Eagle Park

305 Eagle Ln
Belen, NM 87002
505-966-2700
For information, contact Belen Parks and recreation at
City of Belen

Eagle Park is open every Monday through Thursday from 8:00am to 5:00pm, Fridays from 8:00am to 5:00pm and on Saturdays from 8:00am until 12:00pm. Athletic fields are available for rent. Playground and canopies are free, unless you want to use electricity, then there is a fee. There is a Veteran's memorial building as well as a memorial edifice, and a skate park. The Belen Community Center is also at the park. It hosts events, has activity rooms for rent, and a weight/exercise gym. There is a fee for using the gym. A membership to the weight room is available. Check the website for information.

315 N 4th St
Belen, NM 87002
505-966-2700

Enjoy a picnic while children play at the park. The Belen Farmer's market is held at Anna Becker Park. It begins June 18, 2021 and will be held every Friday from 4:00 to 7:00pm through October 29, 2021. Visitors may purchase locally grown eggs, honey and produce.
