About the Chamber of Commerce

Our Purpose:

Welcome to Belen (pronounced beh-len, emphasis on len).

One of the most frequently asked questions in Belen is, "Red or green?" referring to how you prefer your chile. One of the second most frequently asked questions is, "What business does your family own?" That's because small family businesses make up most of the commerce in our city. Our goal is to provide a networking system of businesses that support other businesses. When businesses do well, everyone wins. The money you spend at a local restaurant is used to pay employees. Those employees go out into the community and purchase goods and services from other local businesses. Therefore, helping one business helps all businesses. The Belen Chamber of Commerce helps to promote businesses in Belen so our economy can be stronger. One example is our "Buy Local" campaign that promotes buying from a small, family-owned business instead of big, national, corporate stores.

New Mexico Travertine

Janice Lardner

New Mexico Travertine is located West of Belen on Camino Del Llano just east of the Double Eagle Airport.

NM Travertine was started in 1984 by Vince Lardner, who passed it on to his son, Tim. Tim Lardner passesd away in 2010, leaving the business to his wife, Janice, and their four children. The quarry is located 20 miles west of Belen. New Mexico was once under water, and calciferous sediments created the tan, marble-like rock. NM Travertine is now a worldwide provider of natural stone products.
